India's Antibiotic Crisis: The Newborn Cost

India uses more antibiotics than almost any other country in the world. This heavy use is creating a huge, quiet disaster: Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR). The results are heartbreaking for the most vulnerable people. Every year, about 60,000 newborn babies in India die because of infections that our medicines can no longer cure. This tragic number makes it clear that AMR is not a problem for the future; it is a deadly public health emergency happening right now that requires fast, focused action.

India's Silent Pandemic: The Threat of AMR

The miracle of antibiotics is now a major global threat. As one of the world's highest consumers of these essential drugs, India's high usage rate often coupled with misuse has accelerated a dangerous new menace: Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR). This is not a future problem; it's a silent, ongoing pandemic already claiming over 700,000 lives every single year. If we fail to intervene, the death toll is tragically projected to reach 1 crore (10 million) people annually by 2050, effectively dismantling the foundations of our modern healthcare system.
